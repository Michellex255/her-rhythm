💡 Tips for Cooking Like You Used To
🥩 Mince / Ground meat → Lentils or crumbled tempeh. Season heavily.
🍗 Chicken pieces → Firm tofu or jackfruit. Marinate overnight.

The 4 Phases — Full Guide
Based on Dr Sims · Dr Crawford · Dr Haver · Dr Wright
🩸 Menstrual Phase — Days 1–5 (avg)
Low estrogen, low progesterone · Prostaglandins shed the lining
🏋️ Training (Dr Sims)You can train! If you feel good, go for it. Low hormone = body can handle stress. Modify only for severe cramps. Never skip strength.
🥗 Nutrition (Dr Haver)Anti-inflammatory priority: omega-3 rich foods, ginger, turmeric. Iron-rich foods (lentils + Vit C). Hydrate extra. Avoid alcohol.
🦴 Dr Wright's NoteEstrogen receptors on joints are low → higher injury risk. Warm up longer. Don't push ACL/knee joints to max load.
🌡️ Supplements (Dr Sims)Day 1–10: supplement iron every other day if low ferritin. Magnesium reduces cramps. Omega-3 reduces prostaglandins (pain).
How you'll likely feel: Energy may be lower on days 1–2 but rises rapidly. Many women actually feel relief and clarity once bleeding starts. Sleep is easier now (progesterone is gone). HRV is climbing. Motivation increases toward day 3–4.

Best for: Steady-state cardio, yoga, Muay Thai (if cramps allow), Zone 2 training. Day 3+ → add heavy lifts as energy returns.
🌱 Follicular Phase — Days 6–13 (avg)
Rising estrogen · Your strongest, most energetic phase
🏋️ Training (Dr Sims + Wright)YOUR POWER WINDOW. Heavy lifting (4×4 protocol per Dr Wright). High-intensity intervals. New PRs. Estrogen is anabolic — it builds muscle. Attack your hardest sessions NOW.
🥗 Nutrition (Dr Crawford + Sims)Higher carb tolerance — use it for HIIT fuel. Protein 1g/lb bodyweight. Estrogen improves insulin sensitivity. Good time to dial in calorie goals. Gut microbiome is optimal.
🧠 Brain & MoodEstrogen boosts serotonin and dopamine. You'll feel sharper, more confident, more social. Best time for hard conversations, pitching clients, creative work, learning new things.
🦴 Dr WrightEstrogen protects joints and improves tendon elasticity. BUT ACL injury risk rises near ovulation — controlled movements under load. Don't sacrifice form for PRs.
How you'll likely feel: Rising energy, optimism, sociability. Sleep is excellent — high HRV, deep slow-wave sleep, easy to fall asleep. Skin often clearer. Libido increasing.

Best for: CrossFit, heavy Muay Thai sparring, new skills, sprints, high-volume strength training. Max output sessions go here.
⭐ Ovulation — ~Day 14 (±2 days)
Estrogen peak → LH surge → egg released · 24-hour window
🏋️ Training (Dr Sims)Estrogen surge is intensely anabolic. Another excellent day for heavy resistance. Power output is at peak. Some women hit personal bests the day of or day after ovulation.
⚠️ Injury Risk (Dr Wright)Estrogen peaks → ligament laxity increases significantly. ACL rupture risk is highest in ovulating women. Controlled form. Don't test max lifts without support. Warm up well.
🥗 NutritionKeep protein high. Zinc-rich foods support egg health (pumpkin seeds, lentils). Vitamin E (almonds, sunflower seeds). Anti-oxidant rich foods protect the egg.
🧠 Mood & EnergyPeak confidence, charisma, and social drive. Testosterone also peaks — boosts assertiveness. You may feel unusually capable. Use this for goals, important meetings, client work.
🌸 Early Luteal Phase — Days 15–22 (avg)
Progesterone rises · Body shifting into build-and-repair mode
🏋️ Training (Dr Sims)Dial it back to moderate intensity. Steady-state cardio, moderate weight training. Body is building endometrial lining — energy is being redirected. Don't fight it. Prioritise form over load.
🥗 Nutrition (Dr Sims + Haver)EAT MORE. Progesterone increases metabolism by 100–300 kcal/day. Increase complex carbs before workouts. More protein — amino acids fuel endometrial tissue building. Don't restrict.
💤 Sleep (Dr Haver + Sims)Progesterone raises core temperature → disrupts sleep. Less deep slow-wave sleep, more light sleep, more wakeful episodes. Magnesium L-threonate at night. Cool room. No screens.
🧠 MoodProgesterone has a calming/sedating effect but can cause brain fog. More inward-focused energy. Good time for deep solo work, creative projects, journaling. Less socialising is fine.
Dr Stacy Sims (Diary of a CEO, 2025): "Women often feel strongest around days 6–14. After ovulation it's important not to punish yourself for lower performance — your body is doing something complex. Eat more, recover more."
🌊 Late Luteal / PMS — Days 23–28 (avg)
Estrogen & progesterone drop · Pro-inflammatory state · Your body asking for care
🏋️ Training (Dr Sims)Lower volume, lower intensity. Mobility, functional strength, recovery work. HRV will be low — don't compare to follicular scores. If energy is there, maintain strength. Don't HIIT every day.
🥗 Nutrition (Dr Haver + Crawford)Anti-inflammatory is critical now: omega-3 (reduce prostaglandins), magnesium (chocolate cravings = real deficiency signal), B6 (mood support), complex carbs to blunt cortisol spike.
💊 Supplements (Dr Sims + Haver)Magnesium glycinate 300–400mg at night (reduces PMS, cramping, and sleep issues). Omega-3 2g/day. B6 50mg. Evening primrose oil for breast tenderness. Calcium 1000mg reduces PMS symptoms.
🧠 Mental Health (Dr Haver)Estrogen drop directly hits serotonin. Anxiety, irritability, low mood are real and physiological — not character flaws. EMDR, journalling, rest. Reduce cortisol: no fasted training, no calorie restriction.
Dr Mary Claire Haver: "The brain changes in the late luteal phase are real. This is the time when women most need to reduce cortisol load — not add to it with fasted workouts or calorie restriction. Feed yourself. Rest. Be kind to your body."

Best activities: Yoga, walking, foam rolling, Muay Thai light technical work (not sparring), Zone 2 cardio, stretching. Prioritise sleep above all.
Perimenopause Awareness 🌺
You're 35 — this starts now. Knowledge is protection.

⚠️ Dr Haver + Dr Wright: Perimenopause Can Start at 35

🩺 Symptoms begin as early as 35–38 with regular cycles still present
🧠 Often shows up first as: anxiety, brain fog, sleep disruption, mood shifts — BEFORE hot flashes
🦴 Bone density starts declining in this decade — 20% loss happens during perimenopause transition (Dr Wright)
💪 Muscle loss accelerates — 50% of total testosterone is lost in the 40s (Dr Wright)
❤️ Estrogen protects the heart — as it fluctuates, cardiovascular risk rises (Dr Haver)
🌡️ Gut microbiome shifts toward a "male-type" profile as estrogen declines — more bloating and GI issues (Dr Haver, ZØOE Nutrition Study)
Watch for these signs NOW:
🌙 Waking at 3–4am for no reason
😰 New anxiety or panic feelings
🧠 Forgetting words or names
🔥 Feeling suddenly hot, then cold
❤️ Heart palpitations during rest
🦴 New joint pain or stiffness
🩸 Shorter cycles (26d instead of 28d)
💧 More bloating than usual
✅ What to do right now (all 4 doctors agree): Lift heavy. Eat 1g protein/lb bodyweight. Take D3 + K2 + Magnesium + Omega-3. Track your cycle. Get a DEXA scan at 40. Build muscle like your life depends on it — because your future independence does.
What the Doctors Say 👩‍⚕️
Dr Stacy Sims — Exercise Physiologist (DOAC Roundtable 2025 + Huberman Lab)
"Women are not small men. Follicular phase is your power window — heavy loads, HIIT, new PRs. Luteal phase: eat more, train smarter. Never train fasted — for women it raises cortisol and kills recovery. Creatine 3–5g daily is the most evidence-backed supplement for women, especially perimenopausal. Pilates is not strength training — lift heavy or you will lose bone and muscle."
Dr Mary Claire Haver — OB/GYN, Menopause Specialist (Huberman Lab + DOAC 2025)
"Muscle is a geroprotective organ — the most important one once your ovaries retire. Anti-inflammatory nutrition isn't optional in perimenopause, it's medicine. Fibre is non-negotiable. Alcohol worsens every symptom. The 'eat less, exercise more' model was built for men. Women in perimenopause need MORE protein, MORE fibre, and LESS restriction."
Dr Vonda Wright — Orthopedic Surgeon (DOAC Roundtable 2025 + Alloy)
"70% of hip fractures happen in women and 30% die within a year. Bone health starts NOW — not at 65. 20 jumps a day. Lift as heavy as your bones allow. 4 sets of 4 reps — HEAVY. Every muscle you build in your 30s is bone protection for your 70s. Musculoskeletal symptoms of menopause — joint pain, arthritis, frozen shoulder — are PREVENTABLE with the right moves now."
Dr Natalie Crawford — Reproductive Endocrinologist (DOAC 2025 + Huberman Lab)
"Your menstrual cycle is a vital sign for your overall health — cardiovascular, metabolic, hormonal. Irregular cycles, short luteal phases, or spotting are signals worth investigating. Protein and fibre are the foundation of hormonal balance. Chronic stress raises cortisol which directly suppresses your reproductive hormones. Your fertility health IS your health — full stop."

Supplements 💊
Your personalised timing schedule — conflicts avoided
☕ BEST TIME FOR YOUR COFFEE
9:00 – 9:30 AM
Wait 90 min after waking — lets your natural cortisol do its job first. Coffee then amplifies (not overrides) your energy. Cut off by 2 PM for good sleep.
⚠️ Don't take iron within 1 hour of coffee — caffeine blocks iron absorption
🌿 Vegetarian Priority Supplements
As a vegetarian, you get non-heme iron (plant-based — harder to absorb). Taking Vit C WITH iron boosts absorption up to 3x. B12 is almost exclusively in animal products — your supplement is essential. Omega-3 from plant sources is mostly ALA; your EPA+DHA supplement fills this critical gap for brain and bone health.
🌅 Morning — With Breakfast (includes fat)
🕐 Mid-Morning — 1.5–2 hrs after breakfast, EMPTY stomach
💡 Iron is best absorbed on empty stomach. Take with small glass of orange juice (Vit C boosts absorption). Keep away from calcium, coffee, and high-fibre foods.
🍽️ Lunch — With a fatty meal
🌆 Evening — With dinner (includes fat)
🌙 Night — Before bed
⚠️ Conflicts to Remember
❌ Iron + Calcium — never together, block each other
❌ Iron + Coffee/Tea — wait 1 hr before/after iron
❌ Vit C + B12 — separate by 2 hrs (Vit C can degrade B12 in high doses)
❌ Iron + high-fibre foods — take away from bran, beans at same time
✅ Iron + Vit C — take TOGETHER (boosts plant iron absorption up to 3x)
✅ D3 + K2 + fat — always together, they work synergistically
✅ Omega-3 + Vit E — Vit E protects Omega-3 from oxidation
✅ Magnesium at night — promotes relaxation + sleep + muscle recovery
DIY Skincare 🌿
Natural recipes you can make at home
Why make your own? No parabens, sulfates, or synthetic fragrance. You know every ingredient. Fraction of the cost. Better for your skin barrier — especially while using actives like retinoid and Vit C.
🫒
Evening Oil Cleanser
Removes SPF + light mascara/eyeliner gently
▼
Ingredients:
3 tbsp Jojoba Oil 1 tbsp Castor Oil 3 drops Lavender Essential Oil Small pump bottle
Method:
1. Mix jojoba and castor oil in pump bottle
2. Add lavender drops — shake well
3. Apply 2-3 pumps to DRY face. Massage 60 seconds in circles
4. Add a little warm water — emulsify (turns milky)
5. Wipe off with warm damp cloth. Follow with your HA serum
✨ This also works as your face massage oil for your Sadie Nardini routine! You already own all these ingredients.
🍯
Morning Honey + Castile Cleanser
Gentle, antibacterial, non-stripping
▼
Ingredients:
2 tbsp Raw Honey 2 tbsp Liquid Castile Soap 1 tsp Jojoba Oil 3 drops Frankincense EO (optional)
Method:
1. Mix all ingredients — gentle stir, don't overmix (will foam)
2. Store in small squeeze bottle. Use within 4 weeks
3. Apply pea-size amount to damp face, massage 30-60 sec
4. Rinse with lukewarm water, pat dry — do NOT rub
5. Follow immediately with HA on damp skin
💡 Honey is antibacterial + humectant. Draws moisture INTO skin while cleaning.
🌹
Rose Water Toner (2 ingredients)
Use before your HA serum AM & PM
▼
Ingredients:
3 parts Rose Water (pure) 1 part Witch Hazel (alcohol-free) 5 drops Lavender EO (optional)
Method:
1. Mix in spray bottle — shake before each use
2. After cleansing, spritz lightly or apply with cotton pad
3. While STILL DAMP — apply your HA serum immediately
4. Keeps up to 3 months at room temp
✨ This is the "damp skin" trick — HA pulls moisture FROM this mist into your skin. Game changer.
🍵
Green Tea Antioxidant Toner
Anti-aging, brightening, anti-inflammatory
▼
Ingredients:
½ cup cooled green tea ¼ cup witch hazel (alcohol-free) 10 drops lavender EO Spray bottle
Method:
1. Brew strong green tea, let cool completely
2. Combine all in spray bottle, shake well
3. Refrigerate — use within 2 weeks
4. Spritz after cleansing before serums
🍵 EGCG in green tea fights UV damage and boosts collagen — perfect partner for your Vit C serum in the morning.
🌾
Oat + Honey Exfoliating Scrub
Use 1–2x per week only
▼
Ingredients:
2 tbsp finely ground oats 1 tbsp raw honey 1 tsp jojoba oil Few drops warm water
Method:
1. Grind oats finely in blender (NO sharp pieces)
2. Mix all ingredients into a paste
3. Apply to damp face — gentle circular motions 60 sec
4. Rinse well, pat dry — do NOT use on same night as retinoid!
⚠️ Never exfoliate the same night you use Retinoid. Wait at least 2 days between.
👁️
Natural Eye Makeup Remover
Removes mascara, eyeliner & eyeshadow gently
▼
Ingredients:
1 tbsp Jojoba Oil (or sweet almond) 1 tbsp Aloe Vera Gel (pure) Small dropper bottle
Method:
1. Mix equal parts jojoba + aloe vera in small dropper bottle
2. Shake before use — they separate naturally
3. Apply 2-3 drops to cotton pad — hold gently on closed eye 10 sec
4. Wipe downward ONCE — never rub or drag the eye area
5. Use a fresh pad for the other eye
💡 Jojoba melts waterproof mascara. Aloe soothes the delicate eye area. No stinging, no tugging.
